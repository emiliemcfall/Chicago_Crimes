Chicago Crime Analysis

“Is it really as bad as all the news say it is?”, said Mary Pattillo, Northwestern University sociologist and IPR associate in a Union Club League of Chicago’s event. Mary centered her discussion around the biggest problem Chicago faces today: crime and violence. In the Chicago news today, we constantly hear about theft and homicides, and police and anti-crime organizations efforts to address these issues. While it is remarkable that Chicago’s murder rate has fallen 20% in the last year, we found that homicides are not actually the biggest amount of sub-crimes in Chicago.

Since we all have personal connections with Chicago, we want to help the city of Chicago and anti-crime organizations with their efforts to reduce crime. Through our rigorous analysis of Chicago crime-based datasets, we have broken down Chicago crime data and identified important trends to help reduce the crime rates for the biggest sub-crimes.

Throughout this website, we will let our data lead the story and convince your respective organizations to reconsider your resource allocation towards theft crime.

Theft trends

In the above line chart the trend for the top 4 subcategories of theft is shown. It is clearly visible how all crimes have decreased from 2008 to 2011, but from around 2011 the trend has stagnated - especially for Retail Theft which even increased a bit again from around 2014.

In the plot below we have visualised the arrest-no arrest ratio for each of these 4 sub crimes. Try to filter for “From Building” and you can see how the ratio between arrest and no arrest marginally decreases. Try instead to filter for “Retail Theft” and see how different the ratio is for this crime type.

Seasonal Analysis

The same pattern is detected from the boxplots. Though, it is seen that the median line of Winter is within interquartile range of both Fall and Spring and therefore not likely to be significantly different from these two seasons. On the other hand, it is clear to see that the box lower interquartile range of Winter is below all other seasons, and the total range of this season is less than all other seasons.

It is known that the Winters i Chicago can get very cold with temperatures all the way down to -20 degrees, which could be an explanation of the differences we see.
